🔍 Overview
After two rounds of interviews, I was thrilled to join the Palantir Launch 2024 Program in Washington, DC. It was an incredible journey where I spent a week at the DC office, participated in panel sessions, connected with coworkers, and learned about the company's diverse roles. I delved into Palantir's AIP and Foundry platforms, culminating in a project that allocated vaccines worldwide using Foundry.

What I love about Palantir is the diversity of backgrounds among the people and the opportunities to work in various fields. My peers were amazing, and I learned so much from them. This experience has been one of the most valuable of the year.

  • Solving Open-ended Problems:
    In my classroom setting, I'm often accustomed to close-ended problems. Even when the tasks are challenging, there's always a clear specification on how to approach the project or solve the problem, and there's typically one correct answer. However, real life is rarely so straightforward, and Palantir taught me how to tackle open-ended problems.

    During the program, we worked on an open-ended problem of allocating vaccine resources to different parts of the world for various diseases. Each disease had its own mortality rate, each vaccine had different efficacy, and multiple labs around the globe were producing these vaccines. Within a limited time, we had to decide on our approach and balance various trade-offs. For example, should we assume that the spread rate of a disease is consistent during an outbreak, or should we allocate vaccines based on country or pure population density?

    These open-ended questions required us to think critically and make decisions rather than simply following instructions. I found this type of problem-solving incredibly stimulating and rewarding.
